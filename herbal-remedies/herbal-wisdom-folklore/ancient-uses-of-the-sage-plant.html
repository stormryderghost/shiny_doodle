Ancient Uses of the Sage Plant

Sage has been revered for its medicinal and spiritual properties for thousands of years. In ancient Greece and Rome, sage was used to treat a variety of ailments, from digestive issues to respiratory problems.

In medieval Europe, sage was considered a cure-all and was used in numerous remedies and preparations. It was also believed to bring wisdom and longevity, which is reflected in its Latin name, Salvia, derived from the word “salvare,” meaning “to save.”

Today, sage is still used in traditional medicine and cooking, and its historical significance continues to be celebrated in various cultures.
